Letter to a
Friend.


Long after he co-founded Alcoholics Anonymous, and many years into his sobriety even Bill Wilson would come to realize although his mystical leap of faith had saved him, it only masked deep psychological vaunerabilites that had never been addressed. In a letter to a friend and in desperation to help, he would honestly reveal his unhealthy dependencies:

"...Suddenly, I realized what the matter was. My basic flaw had always been dependence, almost absolute dependence, on people or circumstances to supply me with prestige, security, and the like. Failing to get these things according to my perfectionist dreams and specifications, I had fought for them. And when defeat came, so did my depression."

Bill Wilson "Emotional Sobriety"
